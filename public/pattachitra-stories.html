Pattachitra: Scrolls of Divine Splendor

Explore the intricate world of Pattachitra, where myths unravel in lines and colors passed down through generations of Odisha and Bengal scroll painters.

Legendary Scrolls

Jagannath’s Rath Yatra

Jagannath’s Rath Yatra is one of the most iconic themes in Pattachitra, capturing the grandeur of the annual chariot procession in Puri, Odisha.

The Divine Journey

The scrolls portray Lord Jagannath along with his siblings Balabhadra and Subhadra being carried in massive, decorated chariots. Devotees crowd the scene, their hands raised in devotion, while musicians, dancers, and priests fill the atmosphere with divine energy.

Visual Spectacle

Pattachitra’s distinct style — bold outlines, flat natural colors, and intricate borders — amplifies the spirit of this celebration. Each chariot, deity, and temple tower is rendered with devotion and architectural precision, making the scroll a vibrant tribute to Odisha’s spiritual heart.

Krishna’s Ras Leela

Krishna’s Ras Leela is a favorite motif in Pattachitra, illustrating the divine play (leela) of Krishna with Radha and the gopis under the moonlit Vrindavan skies.

Dance of Devotion

The scrolls capture the circular formations of the dance, with Krishna appearing between each gopi, symbolizing his omnipresence. Expressions of love, grace, and divine ecstasy radiate through the detailed faces and flowing garments.

Nature in Harmony

Lotus ponds, flowering trees, peacocks, and swirling patterns surround the dancers — nature itself seems to join the celebration. The composition reflects spiritual harmony, where music, art, and love unite in a divine rhythm.

Ram’s Return to Ayodhya

Ram’s Return to Ayodhya is a triumphant moment from the Ramayana, celebrated through layers of architectural elegance and joyous detail in Pattachitra scrolls.

The Victorious Arrival

Lord Ram, along with Sita and Lakshman, is shown returning in an ornate aerial chariot as citizens of Ayodhya line the streets with lamps and garlands. Women perform aarti, children dance, and elders shed tears of joy — all intricately painted in festive detail.

Symbol of Dharma

The narrative honors the return of dharma (righteousness), depicting Ram as the ideal king. Golden temple domes, patterned floor designs, and ceremonial arches create a grand visual of restoration and divine justice.

Narayana’s Cosmic Form

Narayana’s Cosmic Form reveals the vastness of Vishnu’s divine presence, filling scrolls with celestial imagery and metaphysical beauty.

Vishwarupa Darshan

In this scene, Lord Vishnu is shown in his cosmic form with countless heads, arms, and divine weapons. Stars, planets, sages, demons, and gods all appear within him — illustrating his all-encompassing nature. The scroll becomes a universe in itself.

Ocean of Existence

Vishnu is often depicted reclining on the serpent Ananta in a cosmic ocean, with a lotus emerging from his navel bearing Brahma. The surrounding scroll is filled with deities, conch shells, and yantras, symbolizing protection, creation, and eternal balance.
