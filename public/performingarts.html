Performing Arts of India

A vibrant tapestry of dance, music, and drama

Explore Indian Performing Arts

Kathakali

A classical Indian dance-drama from Kerala, known for its elaborate makeup, colorful costumes, and detailed gestures.

Kathakali is a highly stylized classical Indian dance-drama originating from Kerala. It is a "story play" genre of art, distinguished by the elaborately colorful make-up, costumes and face masks that the traditionally male actor-dancers wear. It is based on Hindu mythology, particularly the Puranas and the great Indian epics.

Bharatanatyam

A major form of Indian classical dance that originated in Tamil Nadu. It is one of the oldest classical dance traditions in India.

Bharatanatyam is a classical Indian dance form that originated in the temples of Tamil Nadu. It is known for its graceful movements, expressive gestures, and intricate footwork. The dance is usually accompanied by Carnatic music and is considered a fire dance, embodying the element of fire in the human body.

Kuchipudi

An indigenous dance form from Andhra Pradesh, distinct for its lively, dramatic character, and blend of dance, music, and acting.

Kuchipudi is one of the eleven major Indian classical dances. It originated in a village named Kuchipudi in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. It is a dance-drama performance, with roots in ancient Hindu Sanskrit text of Natya Shastra. It typically begins with an prolongation, followed by a presentation of the drama's characters and then the pure dance.

Odissi

An ancient classical dance form from Odisha, known for its fluid movements, sculpturesque poses, and spiritual themes.

Odissi is a classical dance form from the state of Odisha in eastern India. It is particularly distinguished by its emphasis on the 'tribhanga' (three-bend) posture and the 'chauka' (square) stance. Odissi is traditionally a dance-drama genre of performance art, with spiritual themes and devotional stories.

Mohiniyattam

A graceful classical dance form from Kerala, characterized by gentle and flowing movements, often performed by women.

Mohiniyattam is an Indian classical dance form that evolved in the state of Kerala. It is known for its elegant and graceful movements, swaying body movements, and subtle expressions. The dance typically tells stories of Hindu mythology, with a strong emphasis on devotion and love.

Yakshagana

A traditional folk theatre form from Karnataka, combining dance, music, dialogue, costume, and stage techniques with a unique style.

Yakshagana is a traditional theatre form popular in coastal Karnataka districts. It combines dance, music, dialogue, costume, make-up, and stage techniques with a unique style and form. The themes are drawn from the Ramayana, Mahabharata, Puranas, and other Hindu epics.
