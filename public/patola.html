About Patola

Patola is a legendary double ikat woven silk textile from Patan, Gujarat, India. Renowned for its vibrant colors, geometric patterns, and intricate craftsmanship, Patola weaving is a painstaking process that can take months for a single saree.

Each Patola is woven by hand, with both warp and weft threads tie-dyed before weaving to create the signature blurred-edge motifs. Traditionally, Patola sarees were prized by royalty and aristocracy, symbolizing status and auspiciousness.

  • Geometric Patterns: Diamonds, flowers, and animal motifs
  • Vibrant Colors: Natural dyes in red, yellow, green, and indigo
  • Double Ikat: Both warp and weft are resist-dyed for perfect alignment
  • Symbolism: Patterns often carry spiritual and cultural meanings
  • Heritage: Patola weaving is a closely guarded family tradition

Patola art is not just a textile—it's a living heritage, preserving centuries of skill, devotion, and beauty in every thread.

Woven Stories

The Royal Weave

Patola sarees were once the exclusive adornment of queens and aristocrats. Each piece was custom-woven, with motifs chosen for their auspicious meanings and family heritage.

The Secret of Double Ikat

The double ikat technique is so complex that only a handful of families in Patan have mastered it. The process requires perfect alignment of dyed threads, resulting in mesmerizing, blurry-edged patterns.

Motifs of Meaning

From elephants and parrots to flowers and geometric shapes, every Patola motif carries a story—of fertility, prosperity, and spiritual protection for the wearer.

Legacy of the Salvi Family

The Salvi family of Patan has preserved the Patola tradition for centuries, passing down their secrets and skills through generations of master weavers.
