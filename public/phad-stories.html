Phad Art: Epic Scrolls of Rajasthan

Journey through the vibrant scrolls of Phad painting, where gods, warriors, and folk legends come alive under the stars of Rajasthan.

Legendary Scrolls

Devnarayan Lila

Devnarayan Lila is the heart of Phad art — an epic scroll narrating the life and miracles of the warrior-god Devnarayan, revered by the Gujjar community of Rajasthan.

The Divine Birth

Devnarayan is believed to be an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Born under divine circumstances, his birth is marked by auspicious symbols like lotuses, elephants, and a celestial conch. These motifs are meticulously painted onto the scroll, representing his divine origin and dharmic purpose.

The Warrior's Journey

The scroll unfolds with scenes of battles, divine interventions, and acts of justice. Each section is performed and sung by the Bhopas, traditional priest-singers, who bring the story to life with music, torches, and devotion. The scroll is not just art — it's a living scripture.

Pabuji ki Phad

Pabuji ki Phad tells the story of Pabuji Rathore, a Rajput hero and folk deity known for his bravery, loyalty, and sacrifice for his people and cattle.

The Oath of Protection

Pabuji’s tale begins with his vow to protect a herd of camels belonging to a Charan woman. True to his word, even on his wedding day, he leaves the ceremony to rescue the camels from enemies — an act of dharma and selflessness.

Heroism and Legacy

His battles, alliances with mystical beings, and ultimate martyrdom are shown in vivid colors — deep reds, rich oranges, and striking greens. Pabuji, atop his horse, becomes the centerpiece of every scroll, symbolizing the timeless spirit of duty and sacrifice.

Ramdev Pir

Ramdev Pir, another folk deity of Rajasthan, is depicted in Phad paintings as a saint-warrior who transcended caste divisions and became a protector of the downtrodden.

Miracles and Devotion

Ramdev Pir's life is filled with miracles: feeding the hungry, healing the sick, and calming disputes. In the scroll, he is shown mounted on a white horse, offering blessings to all — Hindus and Muslims alike — portraying Phad art's inclusive and spiritual depth.

The Spiritual Message

Through his story, the scroll conveys themes of equality, compassion, and service. Each scene includes devotees, animals, saints, and celestial beings — uniting heaven and earth in a singular visual song.

Galji Bhairav

Galji Bhairav is a fierce protector spirit worshipped in parts of Rajasthan, and his tale is powerfully depicted in rare Phad scrolls filled with drama and symbolism.

The Guardian Deity

Known for his quick justice and wrath against evil-doers, Bhairav is shown with intense eyes, weapons, and animals like dogs — his trusted companions. The paintings illustrate village disputes and Bhairav’s divine retribution, often acting as a social commentary on morality and justice.

Folk Ritual and Belief

These scrolls are not merely art pieces — they are brought out during temple fairs and village ceremonies where Bhairav is invoked. The presence of flames, red outlines, and dancing figures turns the scroll into a dramatic theatre of faith.
