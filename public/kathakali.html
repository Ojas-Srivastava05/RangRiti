About Kathakali

Kathakali, originating from Kerala, is a classical Indian dance-drama characterized by elaborate makeup, costumes, and storytelling through gestures and expressions.

Performances typically depict tales from Hindu epics and Puranas, emphasizing the eternal battle between good and evil. The dancers, all male, undergo rigorous training to master the complex movements and facial expressions that convey the narrative.

Key elements of Kathakali include:

  • Makeup: Distinctive facial makeup that signifies the character's nature
  • Costumes: Elaborate and colorful attire that enhances the visual spectacle
  • Music: Traditional percussion instruments that set the rhythm and mood
  • Gestures: Highly stylized hand gestures (mudras) that communicate the story

Kathakali is not just a dance form but a complete theatrical experience that immerses the audience in a world of myth and legend.

Sacred Stories

The Story of Narakasura

Kathakali often portrays the tale of Narakasura, a demon king defeated by Lord Krishna. The performance highlights the triumph of good over evil through intense battle scenes and vivid expressions.

The Legend of Daksha Yaga

The story of Daksha Yaga, where Lord Shiva's consort Sati immolates herself, is a powerful narrative in Kathakali. The performance showcases the intensity of grief and the cosmic repercussions of disrespect.

The Battle of Kiratamarjuniyam

The epic encounter between Arjuna and Lord Shiva disguised as a hunter is a popular Kathakali theme. The performance emphasizes the themes of devotion, strength, and divine intervention.

The Tale of Keechaka Vadham

The story of Keechaka Vadham, where Bhima avenges Draupadi's humiliation, is a thrilling Kathakali narrative. The performance showcases the themes of justice, revenge, and the power of righteousness.
