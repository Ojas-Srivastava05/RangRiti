About Marble Inlay

Marble Inlay Art is a traditional decorative technique where semi-precious stones are set into marble to form intricate designs, especially floral and geometric motifs.
1. Historical Roots
This art traces back to Pietra Dura in Renaissance Italy and was introduced to India by the Mughals in the 16th century.
2. Mughal Influence
It flourished under Emperor Shah Jahan, with the Taj Mahal standing as the most iconic example of marble inlay craftsmanship.
3. Materials & Technique
Artisans use white marble and inlay stones like lapis lazuli, turquoise, malachite, and jasper, carefully carved and polished by hand.
4. Present-Day Practice
Still practiced in Agra, this heritage art survives through generations of craftsmen creating decor, tabletops, and souvenirs.
